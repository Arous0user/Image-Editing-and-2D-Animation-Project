Topic 2: Working with Layers & Selections

Background

Layers, Masks, and Blending Modes

Layers are like transparent sheets stacked on top of each other. They allow you to work on one element of an image without disturbing the others.

Masks are used to selectively hide or reveal parts of a layer. This is a non-destructive way to edit, as you can always go back and change the mask.

Blending Modes control how a layer's pixels blend with the pixels of the layer below it. Examples include Multiply, Screen, and Overlay.

Selections and Transformations

Selection tools allow you to isolate a specific part of an image to work on. Once you have a selection, you can apply adjustments, filters, or other effects to just that area.

Transformations (scale, rotate, flip, distort) allow you to change the size, orientation, and perspective of a layer or selection.

Experiment Task

Design a poster by combining multiple images and text using several layers. Use blending modes for creative effects.
