Topic 4: Principles of Animation

Background

Core Animation Principles

Timing: The speed of an action, which gives meaning to movement, both in terms of physics and emotion.

Squash and Stretch: Gives a sense of weight and flexibility to drawn objects. The most important principle to create a feeling of life.

Anticipation: The preparation for an action. It makes the action more realistic and easier to follow.

Ease In and Ease Out: Also known as "slow in and slow out," this principle makes movement look more natural by having it start slowly, accelerate, and then slow down before stopping.
